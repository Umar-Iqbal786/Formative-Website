What is climate change?

World temperatures are rising because of human activity and climate change now affects every aspect of human life

Climate change is a change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.

What impact does climate change have?

Nearly all areas are seeing heat waves and hotter days, with 2020 being one of the hottest years in recorded history. Higher temperatures allow for wildfires to start easier and spread faster while also increasing heat-related illness.

Soon, some places will be uninhabitable. Not just because of the increase in temperature but could be through with extreme rainfall causing historic flooding - as seen recently in China, Germany, Belgium and the Netherlands.

Statisitcs

Since 1970, the globe has been warming at an average rate of 0.3 degrees celcius per decade.

Temperature rises must slow down if we want to avoid the worst consequences of climate change, scientists say. They say global warming needs to be kept to 1.5C by 2100 and unless further action is taken, the planet could warm by more than 2C by then.

Scientists say that if nothing is done, global warming could exceed 4C which would lead to devestating heat waves, irreversible loss of plant and animal species and the destruction of millions of homes to rising sea levels.
